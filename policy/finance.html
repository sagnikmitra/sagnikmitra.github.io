The picture

Five numbers set the task

3

The fifty-year slide. Per-capita income rank among states: 3rd in 1960-61, 24th in 2023-24.[2] Share of India's GDP: 7.9% in 1990-91, 5.6% in 2023-24.[2] On the government's own Annual Survey of Industries, West Bengal sits outside the top five states on every industrial measure, and its share of factory value added has slipped from about 3.0% to about 2.5% since 2010-11.[3] Cumulative FDI is about 1.95 billion dollars against Maharashtra's 94 billion.[4]

30.9%

The receipts plan is a transfers plan. The Budget needs revenue receipts to grow 30.9% in one year (₹2,44,867 crore revised to ₹3,20,485 crore budgeted).[1] The composition, from the state's own budget documents: own tax up 16.94%, central grants up 223.5%, from ₹22,069 crore to ₹71,393 crore.[5] Own tax has actually grown 9.98% a year over nine years, with a buoyancy of 1.03 against state GDP growth of 9.69%.[6]

65

Fiscal room is narrow. Salaries, pensions and interest take about 65 paise of every revenue rupee on actuals; the all-states figure is in the low fifties.[7][8] Debt service takes 30.5% of budgeted receipts (₹44,607 crore of repayment plus ₹53,284 crore of interest). Of ₹95,388 crore of fresh gross borrowing, 46.8% goes to repay old principal.[1][5] The CAG has separately reported ₹2.29 lakh crore of grants without utilisation certificates.[9]

57.69%

Spending capacity, not allocation, has been the binding constraint. In audited years, 57.69% of a ₹3,002 crore MGNREGA central-share provision, 64.84% of a Krishak Bandhu provision and 62% of a rice-distribution provision went unspent.[10] Every recommendation below therefore names its delivery machinery, not just its outlay.

89 lakh

The enterprise base is credit-starved and it is measurable. About 89 lakh MSMEs, the largest count of any state, receive bank credit of roughly ₹1.63 lakh per unit per year (₹1.45 lakh crore across the base).[11] Priority-sector achievement under the Annual Credit Plan is 60%. The state credit-deposit ratio is 70.59% on the bankers' committee basis, but five districts sit 22 to 25 points below it: South 24 Parganas 45.54%, Jhargram 46.56%, Birbhum 46.68%, Hooghly 47.18%, North 24 Parganas 48.05%.[12]

The proposals

12 recommendations, each on a commitment the Budget already makes

Every card carries the commitment, the measured baseline, the mechanism, the evidence, the cost, the success measure, its own chart, and the Viksit Bengal impact.

Recommendation 1

Legislate a mid-year revenue report and build a GST analytics cell

Budget commitment. the consolidation path and public financial management reforms [1, p.6]
Baseline. receipts must grow 30.9% right after a year in which the revised estimate itself fell 8.0% short of budget; the 2024-25 SGST revision missed by ₹1,464 crore before an 8.5% target was set for 2025-26; there is no public mid-year tracking [1][24]
Recommendation. a statutory report tabled by 30 November covering every receipt head including central transfers; a directorate cell cross-matching e-way bills with FASTag movement and PAN-linked filings
Reasoning. slippage caught in month six can be corrected; slippage discovered in month twelve lands on capital outlay, which is what the CAG record shows happening [9][10]
Evidence. Maharashtra's analytics enforcement lifted GST 14.6% to ₹1.87 lakh crore, a fourth straight year above target; Telangana's toll-data matching exposed a ₹100 crore fake-invoice racket [25][26]
Cost and funding. ₹40-80 crore, departmental budget; every 0.3 percentage point of extra own tax is worth about ₹6,400 crore a year
Measure of success. report tabled on time; own tax to GSDP moving from 5.5% toward the 6.8% all-states average [8]
Planned growth vs the own-tax record [1][5][6]
Reading. The gap between plan and record is the case for measuring receipts at mid-year
The variance record, and what enforcement is worth [1][24][25]
Reading. The misses are measured; the enforcement upside is measured; the mid-year report connects them
Viksit Bengal impact. revenue credibility funds every Shakti; a published report is the Budget's own transparency pledge made operational
Recommendation 2

Convert Banglar Udyam into a credit-guarantee corpus with a district campaign

Budget commitment. the Banglar Udyam Credit Card, ₹200 crore for about 2 lakh youth, up to ₹10 lakh each [1, p.55]
Baseline. ₹200 crore divided by 2 lakh youth is ₹10,000 a head as a direct benefit; MSME credit runs at about ₹1.63 lakh per unit per year; Annual Credit Plan priority-sector achievement is 60% [11][12]
Recommendation. hold the ₹200 crore as first-loss cover co-guaranteed with CGTMSE; set district credit-deposit targets through the bankers' committee; pilot in the five lowest districts named above; add Common Facility Centres in the jute, handloom, leather and castings clusters under the MSE-CDP window that grants 70 to 90% of project cost
Reasoning. a guarantee multiplies public money ten to fifteen times; a grant divides it; at 10% first-loss cover the same corpus backs ₹2,000 crore of lending, and a 5% default draw of ₹100 crore stays within the corpus
Evidence. CGTMSE has guaranteed ₹5 lakh crore across 76 lakh small enterprises [27]; the cluster window is a standing central scheme [28]
Cost and funding. cost neutral; the ₹200 crore is re-instrumented, not increased
Measure of success. worst-five district ratios up 8-10 points in 24 months; ₹2,000 crore of guaranteed lending disbursed
The re-instrumentation arithmetic [12][27]
Reading. The same ₹200 crore under two designs; guarantee cover set at 10 percent
The enterprise base the guarantee must reach [11][12]
Reading. The measured credit starvation of the base the guarantee corpus is built for
Viksit Bengal impact. the 89-lakh enterprise base, the Budget's own backbone of the state economy, becomes fundable at scale
Recommendation 3

Give the single window a statutory clock

Budget commitment. the single-window pledge and the exemption of ₹100 crore-plus projects from separate local-body clearances, processed in a time-bound manner [1, p.58]
Baseline. the pledge has no statutory deadline or consequence attached yet; the Expert Committee reports in four months
Recommendation. legislate deemed approval: fifteen working days for mega projects, thirty for the rest, automatic approval when the clock lapses, administrative accountability for delay, the portal as the single legal record
Reasoning. after Singur, where the Supreme Court set aside the acquisition in 2016, certainty is the one commodity an investor cannot buy; a statutory clock supplies it [29]
Evidence. Telangana wrote deemed approval into law in 2014; Tamil Nadu grew electronics exports from 1.9 to 14.7 billion dollars in four years on single-window machinery with dedicated investor desks; Vietnam's one-stop provinces draw over 38 billion dollars of FDI a year [30][31][32]
Cost and funding. ₹50-75 crore, within the ₹5,000 crore framework
Measure of success. median days to full clearance at or under thirty, published quarterly
The precedent trajectory [31]
Reading. Four years under single-window machinery
The credibility gap in cumulative FDI equity [4]
Reading. Cumulative FDI equity; the confidence deficit a statutory clock is designed to repair
Viksit Bengal impact. contractual certainty restores the investment credibility the state has lacked since Singur
Recommendation 4

Publish the land bank; pair it with the Urban Land Ceiling white paper

Budget commitment. a comprehensive land bank reclaiming unutilised PSU land, with resumption and refund of lease premium already specified; a re-examination of the Urban Land Ceiling Act, which the Budget itself calls a major investment bottleneck [1, pp.56, 59]
Baseline. no public register, allotment ledger or codified resumption procedure exists yet
Recommendation. a GIS-tagged register with encumbrance status; a public, CAG-auditable allotment ledger; a codified resumption procedure of notice, hearing, refund and re-pooling; the ceiling-act review published as a white paper with transitional provisions
Reasoning. idle public land is the cheapest industrial input the state owns; transparent process is what makes resumption survive litigation
Evidence. Telangana has developed about 28,000 acres across 152 parks through a web-based single window and has lawfully resumed 1,964 acres from about 225 allottees; the CAG's criticism of its remaining opacity is the design lesson to build in from day one [33]
Cost and funding. ₹80-120 crore for digitisation; returns via lease premiums and rent
Measure of success. every parcel visible online; first resumptions completed without adverse court orders
The working precedent [33]
Reading. Resumption survives litigation when the process is published
The legal arc the white paper must close [1][29]
Reading. Fifty years of land-law history is the argument for a published, codified procedure
Viksit Bengal impact. idle public land becomes productive capacity through a process every citizen can audit
Recommendation 5

Set a capital-outlay floor of 2.5% of GSDP, financed by the Budget's own SASCI target

Budget commitment. capital outlay up 54.8% to ₹40,930 crore; a named ₹10,000 crore target under the Centre's fifty-year interest-free capital loans, with the qualifying reforms listed; a pipeline including the Dandanpatrabarh deep-sea port [1, p.18]
Baseline. outlay is about 1.9% of GSDP against a 2.2 to 2.4% band across states; in an audited year 58.84% of market borrowing financed the revenue account [9][34]
Recommendation. a binding floor of 2.5% of GSDP written into the FRBM statement; full drawdown of the ₹10,000 crore against the named reform checklist; a rolling three-year capital plan
Reasoning. the national institute's estimate puts the capital-spending multiplier at 2.45 against roughly 1.0 for revenue spending; an extra ₹12,900 crore of outlay implies about ₹31,600 crore of output [35]
Evidence. fourteen states already fund more than a quarter of their capital outlay from these interest-free loans [8]; the port arithmetic shows the stakes: Mundra moved 200.7 million tonnes last year, three times Kolkata port's best-ever 66.4, while Haldia's 8.5-metre draft costs about ₹400 crore a year in dredging [36]
Cost and funding. financed by the central loan window; near-zero interest cost
Measure of success. outlay at or above 2.3% of GSDP within 24 months; ₹10,000 crore drawn
Outlay position and target [34][35]
Reading. Capital spending returns 2.45 against about 1.0 for revenue spending
The port gap behind the capital pipeline [36]
Reading. Cargo handled, million tonnes; Haldia's 8.5 m draft costs about ₹400 crore a year in dredging
Viksit Bengal impact. capital spending is the strongest growth instrument the state holds; the floor protects it from bearing corrections
Recommendation 6

Create a Geographical Indications monetisation cell for the 59 tags

Budget commitment. the PM Ekta Mall (₹150 crore), the Common Tea Processing Centre (₹100 crore), the tea-tourism land-cap correction, and the Durga Puja global branding campaign [1]
Baseline. about 35 registered tags as of 2025, among the larger state portfolios, with no unified enforcement or brand machinery; most tea sold worldwide as Darjeeling is not genuine; protected handloom articles are openly produced on powerlooms [37][38]
Recommendation. one state cell: testing infrastructure on the import corridor, producer collectives linked to export channels, a single Bengal GI mark launched through the Durga Puja campaign, and enforcement task forces in the named weaving belts
Reasoning. an enforced tag changes the producer price, not just the label: authentic Darjeeling earns 15 to 40 dollars a kilogram against 2.50 to 3.50 for generic black tea [37]
Evidence. France's appellation system protects 42 billion euros of sales a year and about 400,000 jobs; Basmati under APEDA and GI earned 5.94 billion dollars in 2024-25 [39][40]
Cost and funding. ₹100-150 crore, partly within the Ekta Mall provision
Measure of success. first enforcement actions; premium capture measured across the tag portfolio
The enforced-tag premium [37]
Reading. Midpoints of 15 to 40 dollars against 2.50 to 3.50; enforcement is the difference
The decay clock on the flagship tag [15]
Reading. Output halved; Nepal imports of 13 to 16 million kg a year exceed twice Darjeeling's crop
Viksit Bengal impact. heritage becomes rural export income, carried on the Budget's own Durga Puja global campaign
Recommendation 7

Track every MoU in public and publish a semiconductor incentive schedule

Budget commitment. the ₹5,000 crore incentive framework, the Durgapur semiconductor ambition, the GCC policy, and a live pipeline: Berger ₹600 crore, Peerless ₹1,100 crore, an L&T data centre with 25,000 jobs, PwC [1]
Baseline. no public conversion tracking exists; competing offers are public: Micron chose Gujarat on 50% central plus about 20% state support of a 2.75 billion dollar project; Tata put ₹27,000 crore into Assam [41][42]
Recommendation. a live dashboard from signature to land to construction to operation, a named conversion officer, a quarterly published ratio; and a published incentive schedule for semiconductor and data-centre projects that matches the Gujarat and Assam stacks
Reasoning. published conversion is both accountability and marketing; an unpublished incentive offer is a silent no-bid
Evidence. Tamil Nadu publishes its grounding record (722 of 897 memoranda, about 80%, ₹10.27 lakh crore) and campaigns on it; the audited counter-example saw renewable-energy memoranda convert at 7.35% [43][44]
Cost and funding. ₹10-20 crore
Measure of success. ratio published quarterly and above 50%
Conversion with and without daylight [43][44]
Reading. 722 of 897 memoranda grounded; publication is both accountability and marketing
The incentive schedule a Durgapur bid competes against [41]
Reading. The published stack that won Micron's 2.75 billion dollar Sanand project; this is the bid to beat
Viksit Bengal impact. a state that grounds what it signs becomes its own best investment advertisement
Recommendation 8

Take property registration fully online

Budget commitment. ₹50 crore for registration reforms: online verification, slot booking, biometric identification, paperless refunds [1, p.6]
Baseline. the reforms are funded but partial; registration remains a large, under-digitised own-revenue head
Recommendation. end-to-end electronic registration and stamping, digitised valuation, registration from developer offices, an 85%-online target
Evidence. Maharashtra collected ₹50,143 crore in 2023-24 with about 85% of registrations online; Karnataka's rebuilt system registers a property in about ten minutes and collects ₹20,287 crore [45]
Cost and funding. within the ₹50 crore already provided
Measure of success. 85% of registrations online; double-digit revenue growth without a rate change
What full digitisation collects [45]
Reading. Kolkata's own registration head is the open opportunity
What end-to-end digitisation looks like in operation [45][1]
Reading. The operating standard the provision should buy
Viksit Bengal impact. own revenue strengthens without touching a single rate, and the citizen experience improves with it
Recommendation 9

Add track-and-trace to excise alongside the new distance rules

Budget commitment. protective distance rules: no licence within a kilometre of schools, hospitals or places of worship, five hundred metres inside Kolkata [1, pp.69-70]
Baseline. the rules restrict outlets, which costs revenue at the margin, and no anti-leakage modernisation offsets it
Recommendation. GPS movement tracking, digital locks, QR-coded bottles, point-of-sale machines, and lottery-based allotment of retail licences
Reasoning. recovering leakage lets the social objective and the exchequer win together, instead of trading one for the other
Evidence. Uttar Pradesh collected a record ₹57,722 crore, up 9.79%, on a digitised chain; Karnataka's price-led attempt lifted rates but beer volumes fell 13.74% and the target was missed, which argues for the leakage route [46][47]
Cost and funding. ₹100-150 crore; a 5 to 8% leakage recovery is worth several hundred crore a year
Measure of success. collections rising while outlet counts fall
The two routes compared [46][47]
Reading. Recovering leakage beats a blunt price increase
Restriction and recovery, side by side [1][46]
Reading. The restriction the Budget chose, and the recovery mechanism that pays for it
Viksit Bengal impact. the social objective near schools and places of worship and the exchequer win together
Recommendation 10

Fund the profession-tax relief by widening the base

Budget commitment. thresholds doubled for salaried and self-employed taxpayers, the levy decriminalised, border force personnel exempted [1, pp.67-68]
Baseline. genuine relief that forgoes revenue in the exact year receipts must jump; no offsetting base-widening measure exists
Recommendation. auto-enrol professionals from GST and e-Shram rolls as thresholds rise; move vehicle tax to the national Vahan platform with lifetime and green-tax components
Evidence. Karnataka's platform-based vehicle tax went from ₹10,611 crore to a budgeted ₹13,000 crore in two years [45]
Cost and funding. ₹20-30 crore; plausibly revenue neutral against the relief
Measure of success. registered taxpayer count rising even as thresholds rise
The base-widening precedent [45]
Reading. Platform-based collection, ₹ crore
The enrolment rolls the base-widening draws on [1]
Reading. The roll from which auto-enrolment widens the base while thresholds rise
Viksit Bengal impact. taxpayer relief and fiscal discipline reconciled in one design
Recommendation 11

Modernise municipal property tax, the largest untapped urban pool

Budget commitment. urban development at ₹14,610 crore and the City Economic Regions scheme [1, p.18]
Baseline. Kolkata collects ₹1,258.53 crore in property tax where Bengaluru collects ₹4,930 crore and Hyderabad ₹2,012 crore; that is 25.5% of Bengaluru's take and 62.6% of Hyderabad's [48]
Recommendation. a GIS property census for Kolkata and the six largest municipal bodies, completion of unit-area assessment, digital billing with early-payment incentives, a published efficiency dashboard per body
Reasoning. urban own revenue is the only sustainable financing for the urban agenda; the comparator gap is a measured opportunity at the ₹3,000 crore-a-year scale, allowing for property-base differences
Evidence. Bengaluru's digital assessment-linked collection is the working model; GIS mapping drives in peer cities lifted assessment coverage by double digits [48]
Cost and funding. ₹100-150 crore for the census
Measure of success. Kolkata collection above ₹2,000 crore in 24 months
Property-tax collections compared [48]
Reading. The comparator gap is the opportunity, allowing for property-base differences
The 24-month collection path the census should buy [48]
Reading. The census turns the comparator gap into a measurable commitment; Bengaluru-level performance is worth over ₹3,000 crore a year
Viksit Bengal impact. self-financing cities are what make the City Economic Regions programme durable
Recommendation 12

Constitute a Calcutta Stock Exchange revival task force

Budget commitment. the stated aim to reclaim Kolkata's place as a financial capital by reviving the 118-year-old exchange, alongside plans to list profitable state enterprises [1, p.57]
Baseline. the exchange sits in a regulatory exit-or-revive process; the pathway of net worth, platform and governance norms remains to be executed [49]
Recommendation. a time-bound task force of the Finance Department, regulator liaison and exchange stakeholders; a capitalisation plan to meet the norms; a focus on SME listings for eastern India; the state's own PSU listings as anchor volume
Reasoning. eastern India lacks a proximate listing venue, and the SME focus matches an 89-lakh-enterprise base
Cost and funding. about ₹5 crore; capitalisation from a consortium, not the budget
Measure of success. regulatory plan approved; first SME listings
Scale of the ask against the asset [49]
Reading. Capitalisation comes from a consortium, not the budget
The listing hinterland a revived exchange serves [11][71]
Reading. The demand side: an enterprise base with no proximate listing venue in eastern India
Viksit Bengal impact. Kolkata's financial-capital identity returns with measurable milestones, not nostalgia
