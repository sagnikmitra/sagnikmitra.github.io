West Bengal Budget 2026-27: Youth Affairs and Sports

Key findings and eleven structured recommendations, on the Yuva Shakti chapter. Every number links to a source at the end.

The picture

Five numbers set the task

77.6 lakh

The stake. The live jobseeker register holds 77.6 lakh names, the largest of any state.[2] Youth unemployment (ages 15 to 29) is 9.0% on the official survey (men 8.5%, women 10.0%) against an all-ages rate of 2.5%: the young carry the adjustment.[3] Informality runs deep: 66.7% of working women are self-employed and 2.64 crore workers are on the e-Shram register.[4]

5,63,911

The talent pipeline. The state produces 5,63,911 graduates a year on the official count, yet enrolment (GER 26.3 against 28.4) and college density (15 per lakh against 30) trail the country.[5] Lower down: 68.7% of schools have playgrounds against 83% nationally, and the secondary dropout rate is 20.3% against 8.2%, roughly two and a half times.[6] The health baseline from NFHS-5: 41.6% of women aged 20 to 24 were married before 18, and 71% of women are anaemic.[7]

49.50

The sports budget line. Spending is ₹49.50 per person against Odisha's ₹284.88, a 5.75-times gap, and the youth and sports line was near-flat before this Budget.[8] Central flows confirm the position: the SAI block grant has run at ₹56 to 89 crore a year and is declining, the state fields 112 Khelo India Athletes, under 4% of the pool, and it is among the ten lowest-funded states for Khelo India infrastructure.[9]

13

The dormant national footprint. The Khelo India State Centre of Excellence is non-operational; all 13 civilian Khelo India Centres sit inside Navodaya schools rather than open community facilities; all 8 railway centres are non-operational; and no West Bengal district is in the current Aspirational Districts list, so no central siting channel exists.[10][11] The Budget's 100-stadium programme is therefore the base layer being built at last, and its ₹5 crore per stadium is adequate against the ₹3.90 crore benchmark for an indoor hall.[12]

379%

Bengal's own evidence on youth support. The randomised trial in Murshidabad that gave poor households a productive asset, a weekly stipend and coaching, published in Science and followed for a decade, roughly doubled earnings and returned 379% of its cost by year ten.[13] Bhorsha Karmasuchi uncapped would cost ₹3,456 to 5,472 crore a year at the eligible pool of 12 to 19 lakh (derived from the register after age and income filters); capped at 2 lakh with a work-or-training condition it costs about ₹575 to 650 crore.[2][14]

The proposals

11 recommendations, each on a commitment the Budget already makes

Every card carries the commitment, the measured baseline, the mechanism, the evidence, the cost, the success measure, its own chart, and the Viksit Bengal impact.

Recommendation 1

Publish the phasing of the sports capital programme

Budget commitment. Sports University ₹150 crore; North Bengal international and indoor stadium ₹20 crore; 100 constituency stadiums at ₹5 crore; Rabindra Sarovar renovation ₹100 crore; prize corpus ₹50 crore [1, pp.30-31]
Baseline. the named projects total about ₹820 crore against a ₹490.54 crore annual sports provision; nothing states which year builds what
Recommendation. a published three-to-five-year schedule; financing from the Khelo India central share, the Budget's own ₹10,000 crore interest-free capital-loan target, and a state tranche; a quarterly completion dashboard
Reasoning. phasing converts announcements into build schedules and protects the programme from the announced-but-unfinished pattern
Evidence. Odisha phases a roughly ₹5,000 crore programme block by block [15]
Cost and funding. about ₹205 crore a year over four years, largely offset by central shares
Measure of success. stadiums completed on the published schedule
The phasing arithmetic [1]
Reading. Phasing brings the programme inside the annual line
What the ₹820 crore is made of (₹ crore) [1]
Reading. The items the phasing schedule must sequence; 150+20+500+100+50 = 820
Viksit Bengal impact. Yuva Shakti becomes visibly deliverable, year by year, stadium by stadium
Recommendation 2

Fund stadium upkeep by sponsorship, not the annual budget

Budget commitment. the 100-stadium programme and ₹1 crore assistance for clubs at national events [1, p.31]
Baseline. no recurring operations provision exists; unmaintained public sports assets decay within three to five years
Recommendation. an Adopt-a-Stadium framework: public-sector and corporate naming rights underwriting ₹15 to 25 lakh a year per stadium, published per-stadium accounts, community-hours conditions
Evidence. Odisha Mining Corporation underwrites Indian hockey with ₹434 crore across the ten-year term to 2033; the Kalinga centre runs on a government-corporate operating partnership [16]
Cost and funding. ₹15-25 crore a year statewide, sponsorship-offset
Measure of success. at least fifty stadiums under sponsored upkeep in 24 months
Sponsorship covers the operations bill [16]
Reading. ₹434 crore over the ten-year term to 2033, about ₹43 crore a year; derived
The unit economics of Adopt-a-Stadium [16]
Reading. The per-stadium economics a sponsor signs up to
Viksit Bengal impact. the stadiums still serve their communities in 2035 instead of decaying by 2030
Recommendation 3

Site the stadiums by a published formula and win Khelo India accreditation

Budget commitment. stadiums in every Assembly constituency [1, p.30]
Baseline. the state centre of excellence is non-operational, the 13 civilian centres sit inside Navodaya schools, the 8 railway centres are non-operational, and no siting logic has been announced [10]
Recommendation. a published formula of population, district income (inverted) and measured facility gap, which places the first cohort in Birbhum, Uttar Dinajpur, Purulia, Malda and Dakshin Dinajpur, with Jhargram surveyed first; a joint plan with SAI to revive the centre of excellence and accredit new stadiums as Khelo India Centres
Reasoning. accreditation converts state construction into centrally supported operations; a published formula is the audit-proof answer to where the hundred go
Cost and funding. near zero; it is siting logic and accreditation paperwork
Measure of success. the centre of excellence live; 25 accredited centres operating
The dormant footprint to activate [10]
Reading. Accreditation turns state construction into supported operations
WB's position in the central sports-funding system [9][11]
Reading. The central-money position accreditation is designed to reverse
Viksit Bengal impact. every stadium lands where the measured gap is largest, and each becomes a centrally supported training node
Recommendation 4

Put a sports-science core inside the university

Budget commitment. the ₹150 crore Sports University with sports-science courses named [1, p.30]
Baseline. no laboratory or analytics core is specified; without one a sports university produces degrees, not podium finishes
Recommendation. ring-fence ₹30 to 40 crore for biomechanics, physiology, a para-athlete laboratory, recovery suites and a data unit that serves every state academy
Evidence. Odisha built exactly this at Kalinga; the JSW institute trains 300 athletes, and JSW-supported athletes took 17 medals at the 2022 Asian Games; the UK's science-led, target-linked funding took Britain from 36th at Atlanta to 2nd at Rio, at about 4.1 million pounds per medal [17][18]
Cost and funding. from within the ₹150 crore
Measure of success. every state academy served by the unit; medal-lane targets published
The reallocation inside the provision [1]
Reading. The share that separates a podium programme from a degree programme
What science-led funding did for Britain [18]
Reading. Olympic medal-table rank, lower is better; science-led, target-linked funding did this at about 4.1 million pounds per medal
Viksit Bengal impact. Bengal starts producing medallists for the Budget's own reward schemes, not only degree-holders
Recommendation 5

Publish the medal-reward matrix now

Budget commitment. posts from Inspector up to Deputy Superintendent for medal winners and a ₹50 crore prize corpus; the policy is being drawn up [1, p.31]
Baseline. athletes cannot plan careers around an unpublished policy; unclear reward regimes leak talent to states with clear ones
Recommendation. a published matrix: fixed cash per medal tier and a defined post per achievement level, enacted with an annual review against outcomes
Evidence. Haryana pays ₹6 crore, ₹4 crore and ₹2.5 crore for Olympic gold, silver and bronze with ₹15 lakh for every participant, maps 224 posts to achievement, and retains its athletes [19]
Cost and funding. anchored to the ₹50 crore corpus already provided
Measure of success. matrix notified; retention of ranked athletes
The precedent matrix [19]
Reading. Haryana's published slab, plus 224 mapped posts; the clarity is the policy
The rest of the precedent: posts and certainty [19][1]
Reading. Beyond the cash slab: the jobs mapping and multi-year certainty that make a career plan
Viksit Bengal impact. athletes plan their careers around Bengal instead of migrating to states with published policies
Recommendation 6

Build block-level talent scouting and convene the Maidan land framework

Budget commitment. mass participation through the Khelo India rollout; club assistance [1]
Baseline. no scouting pipeline exists beneath the new infrastructure; the historic clubs' grounds sit on defence-administered Maidan land where permanent construction needs case-by-case clearance, one structural reason academies lag a fan base that set India's record crowd of 1,31,781 [20][21]
Recommendation. hostels for scouted children aged 10 to 14, a digitised talent portal for ages 8 to 17, one nursery per block through the block offices; and a coordination framework among the state, the defence land authorities and the clubs for long-lease academy infrastructure under the applicable rules
Evidence. Odisha has run sports hostels since 1985 and block-level talent search; West Bengal holds 33 Santosh Trophy titles, the most of any state, so the demand side is proven [22][23]
Cost and funding. ₹50-75 crore phased; the framework costs convening effort, not money
Measure of success. one lakh children screened; first club academy under construction
Football pedigree against the academy gap [23]
Reading. The demand and the heritage are proven; the pipeline is the missing piece
The demand is already in the stands [21]
Reading. India's record attendance for any sport; the audience the block-level pipeline would feed
Viksit Bengal impact. the state that set India's record crowd builds the pipeline its fans deserve, district by district
Recommendation 7

Grow the startup funds to peer scale on a five-year path

Budget commitment. ₹40 crore incubation and ₹60 crore venture funds, a ₹50 crore deep-tech talent fund, a thousand Atal Tinkering Labs [1, pp.26, 56]
Baseline. the state holds 4,627 recognised startups against Maharashtra's 25,044 and Karnataka's 15,019; reported venture funding into Kolkata over a decade, about 250 million dollars, is of the order of what Bengaluru attracts in a month [24][25]
Recommendation. a five-year path from ₹100 crore to a ₹500 crore fund-of-funds co-investing with registered funds; a grant track of ₹25 to 50 lakh for idea-to-prototype; delivery through college incubators riding the new school labs
Evidence. Gujarat's ₹500 crore student-startup corpus funded 1,543 startups through 339 institutions; Kerala anchors 8,000 startups on 550 campus incubators [26]
Cost and funding. year one cost neutral; ₹80-100 crore a year thereafter
Measure of success. recognised startups above 7,500 in 24 months
The recognised-startup gap, June 2024 [24]
Reading. Counts as on 30 June 2024; every state has grown since, so the gap is the point
The capital gap behind the startup gap [25]
Reading. The funding drought the five-year fund-of-funds path is designed to break
Viksit Bengal impact. the founder exodus reverses on local capital plus Kolkata's cost-of-living advantage
Recommendation 8

Bridge the skilling money with one certification and placement platform

Budget commitment. Technical Education ₹2,393 crore, ITI modernisation ₹570 crore, AVGC-XR ₹50 crore training one lakh youth a year, free district coaching, the GCC policy, Self-Employment ₹323 crore: about ₹3,827 crore in total [1]
Baseline. the rails run separately; no unified certification, no placement tracking, no employer co-governance; the migration driver in every account is job quality [27]
Recommendation. one platform: nationally recognised certificates, employer-designed curricula, paid in-firm training on the dual model, published placement rates, and the GCC policy pointed at it as the demand side
Evidence. Germany's employer-led system, with about 430,000 participating firms, retains 68% of trainees at their training firm; Kerala has certified over 2.5 lakh students on one accredited platform; Singapore measures 55% re-employment within six months for previously unemployed trainees [28][29]
Cost and funding. ₹50-75 crore, pooled from the three departments
Measure of success. 60% of certified youth placed within six months, published
The stack one platform would bridge [1]
Reading. About ₹3,827 crore, today running on separate rails
What measured, employer-led systems deliver [28][29]
Reading. The outcome benchmarks the single platform is being built to hit
Viksit Bengal impact. certified, placed and retained youth, with the Budget's own GCC policy as the demand side
Recommendation 9

Launch Bhorsha Karmasuchi as cash plus activation, with a published cap

Budget commitment. ₹3,000 a month for unemployed graduates and ₹2,000 for others, ages 21 to 45, family income below ₹1 lakh, from October 2026 [1, p.32]
Baseline. no allocation is attached in the Annual Financial Statement; the register holds 77.6 lakh names; the filtered eligible pool is 12 to 19 lakh; uncapped cost runs ₹3,456 to 5,472 crore a year, seven to eleven times the sports provision [1][2][14]
Recommendation. publish a cap derived from the register; attach a work-or-training condition; guarantee an offer of work, training or apprenticeship within four months of enrolment; run a 50,000-person pilot of the Murshidabad graduation design (asset, stipend, coaching); write in an outcome-linked review
Reasoning. across more than 200 evaluations of labour-market programmes, training-centred designs outperform pure transfers at two-to-three-year horizons; the four-month activation offer is the design the European guarantee scaled to 63 million young people [30][31]
Evidence. the Murshidabad trial roughly doubled microenterprise earnings by year seven, with incomes 38% higher and a 379% benefit-cost at year ten [13]; Rajasthan runs its comparable allowance capped at 2 lakh with an internship condition at about ₹866 crore a year; the state's own earlier Yuvasree scheme was capped at one lakh [32]
Cost and funding. capped and conditioned: about ₹575-650 crore a year; the pilot about ₹250-300 crore once
Measure of success. earnings of participants, measured at midline, not enrolment counts
Cost by design choice [14]
Reading. The sports provision is ₹490.54 crore; only the capped design sits near it
From register to cap: the eligibility funnel [2]
Reading. From register to cap, in lakh people; Yuvasree, the state's own precedent, was capped at 1 lakh
Viksit Bengal impact. a support payment becomes a compounding asset, measured in participant earnings
Recommendation 10

Anchor AVGC-XR in a Centre of Excellence with placement

Budget commitment. ₹50 crore to train about one lakh youth a year, 500 Content Creator Labs, the WEBEL academy revamp [1, p.30]
Baseline. training exists, an industry destination does not; Kolkata has one major studio and a film industry of 145 releases a year, and the roughly 3,200 artists displaced by the Technicolor closure worked in Bengaluru and Mumbai, talent the state can attract [33]
Recommendation. a physical Centre of Excellence with studios, incubation and industry residency; a local-hire incentive; a permanent content agency for funding, export promotion and an annual data report; a talent-attraction window aimed at the displaced artists
Evidence. India's animation and gaming market is projected from about 3 to over 26 billion dollars by 2030 with direct jobs from 2.6 to about 23 lakh; Telangana's dedicated policy built roughly 30,000 VFX jobs; Korea's state content agency anchors an 87,000-job games industry [34][35]
Cost and funding. within the ₹50 crore plus studio co-investment; the agency ₹10-15 crore a year
Measure of success. placement rate of trainees; studios signed into the centre
The market the trainees need a door into [34]
Reading. Direct jobs projected from 2.6 to about 23 lakh by 2032
The industry base to build on [35]
Reading. Precedent employment, a displaced-talent window, and the local base the centre anchors
Viksit Bengal impact. Tollygunge's storytelling heritage industrialises into modern creative jobs
Recommendation 11

Fund a women-in-sports pipeline and one signature annual event

Budget commitment. the largest women's programme provision in the state's budget to date (₹36,000 crore Annapurna, ₹1,000 crore for girl undergraduates, free bus travel), stadium assets, and the Durga Puja global branding campaign [1]
Baseline. no women-specific sports provision exists; female labour-force participation is 33.8%; the state draws 18.5 crore tourist visits a year, second among states for foreign visitors, with no sports-event economics attached [4][36]
Recommendation. scholarships, hostels, coaching and reserved scouting slots for girls, with published participation ratios as a condition on every state sports grant; one signature annual event, a riverfront heritage marathon or a Darjeeling circuit, designed for multi-night visitors and cross-marketed with the Durga Puja campaign, and measured on incremental spend rather than footfall claims
Evidence. women delivered 13 of Australia's 18 golds in Paris after record women's-sport investment; Title IX grew American girls' participation roughly tenfold by making access a funding condition; Singapore's Grand Prix has banked 2.2 billion Singapore dollars of cumulative incremental receipts from 720,000 international visitors since 2008 [37][38][39]
Cost and funding. ₹30-50 crore; the event runs on stadium capital already budgeted
Measure of success. published girls'-participation ratios rising; event visitor-nights measured
The highest-return medal lane [37]
Reading. After record women's-sport investment; access rules did the same in US schools
The event-economics case, on assets already here [36][39]
Reading. The tourism-revenue case for the signature event, measured on incremental spend
Viksit Bengal impact. equity and tourism revenue flow from stadium capital already budgeted, a double return
